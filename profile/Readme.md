## What is Ananta Neural?

Ananta Neural is an independent AGI research lab focused on building **MAYA** (our internal AGI system) from first principles — grounded in neuroscience, not just engineering.

We believe the path to true AGI does not run through scaling transformers. It runs through understanding how biological intelligence actually works.

---

## The MAYA Project

MAYA is our core research system. Unlike conventional AI, MAYA is designed around three foundational principles:

**1. Predictive Processing, Not Pattern Matching**
The brain is not a passive data absorber — it is a generative prediction machine. MAYA's architecture is built on active inference and the Free Energy Principle (Friston), where the system continuously minimizes prediction errors rather than matching patterns.

**2. Biologically Plausible Learning**
We reject backpropagation as the core learning mechanism. Neurons communicate locally and adapt instantly. MAYA is being built using Hebbian learning, Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), and continuous online learning — solving the catastrophic forgetting problem at its root.

**3. Embodied & Grounded Intelligence**
Intelligence cannot exist in a vacuum. MAYA must be grounded in a controlled environment where actions have tangible consequences — not just fed text and data.

## Core Architecture

```
MAYA Architecture
├── The Neocortex       → Complex pattern recognition, reasoning, high-level abstraction
├── The Hippocampus     → Episodic memory, fast one-shot learning, past experience replay
└── The Basal Ganglia   → Action selection, reward processing (dopamine/serotonin simulation)
```

The system runs on a **Curiosity-Driven Reward Engine** — positive reward for novel pattern discovery, negative reward (urgency/pain signal) for idle or failing states.

> The brain did not evolve to solve math or write philosophy. It evolved to survive and navigate a complex physical world. A human child is not born with high-level intelligence — they are born with basic reflexes, sensory inputs, and a reward/pain system. Intelligence emerges from interaction.
